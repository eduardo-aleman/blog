summary: >
  John Stuart Mill's seminal essay defending individual freedom against
  the tyranny of society and government.
arguments:
  - label: "The Harm Principle"
    detail: >
      The only legitimate basis for society or government to exercise power
      over an individual is to prevent harm to others. Self-regarding actions
      must remain beyond interference.
  - label: "The Tyranny of the Majority"
    detail: >
      Democratic majorities can suppress minority opinion and lifestyle as
      effectively as any despot; liberty therefore requires protection from
      social coercion, not just from the state.
  - label: "Freedom of Thought and Discussion"
    detail: >
      No opinion should be silenced, even a false one, because open debate
      is the only reliable mechanism for testing truth and preventing the
      calcification of received doctrine.
  - label: "Individuality as a Social Good"
    detail: >
      The free development of individual character and experiment in living
      benefits society as a whole, not merely the individual concerned.
  - label: "The Limits of Paternalism"
    detail: >
      Individuals are the best judges of their own interests; paternalistic
      intervention — however well-intentioned — undermines both autonomy
      and human development.
